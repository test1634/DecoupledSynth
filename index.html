    Overall Architecture of DecoupledSynth.

    It consists of three stages: (A)Decoupled Prompt Extracting Stage, (B)Text-to-Decom Features Stage, (C)Decom Features-to-Speech Stage.

    Abstract:

      Studies of speech representation enhance zero-shot Text-to-Speech by mapping text to intermediate representations before generating speech. However, currently used representations often struggle to balance linguistic, para-linguistic, and non-linguistic information in speech during synthesis phase. Additionally, it often takes substantial resources for representation extraction training. To address these limitations, we propose DecoupledSynth. It combines different self-supervised models to extract comprehensive, decoupled representations. This structural also enables more comprehensive and nuanced synthesis by leveraging reference speech with decoupled processing stages. Experiments on the VCTK and LibriTTS datasets support the potential of this new framework, showing that it can produce more consistent and realistic speech.

    Experiments on VCTK

  • The VCTK Corpus includes around 44 hours of speech data uttered by 110 English speakers with various accents. Each speaker reads out about 400 sentences.
  • Ten speakers are randomly selected from the VCTK dataset as the test set, including 5 women and 5 men.
  • On the VCTK dataset, DecoupledSynth was compared with StyleTTS(using HiFi-GAN vocoder), VITS, YourTTS, and VALL-E.
  • For VITS, we adapted it to a zero-shot scenario by integrating the ECAPA-TDNN model to extract speaker embedding.
  • The first 2 to 3 seconds of the ground truth speech are extracted as reference speech, with the precise duration determined by the Voice Activity Detection (VAD) cutoff point. Care is taken to ensure that the extracted segment ends at the completion of a full word pronunciation.

    Ablation study

  • An ablation study was conducted to verify the effectiveness of the Decom Features and decoupled prompts of reference speech in DecoupledSynth.
  • For Decom Features ablation, we use Hubert features and extract timbre information using a style encoder, which are common choices for traditional zero-shot TTS speech synthesis. It is called "-w/o Decom"
  • For decoupled prompts of reference speech ablation, we extract the single prompt of the reference speech using a unified style encoder. It is called "-w/o DeRef"
  • The experimental setup was consistent with the comparison experiments.
  • The first 2 to 3 seconds of the ground truth speech are extracted as reference speech, with the precise duration determined by the Voice Activity Detection (VAD) cutoff point. Care is taken to ensure that the extracted segment ends at the completion of a full word pronunciation.

    Validation of Decom Features Decoupling Capabilities

  • We proposed DecoupledSynth. This work improves the performance of zero-shot TTS by decoupling the speech. In addition, relying on our decoupled representations of decom features, we can also achieve the embedding of rhymes in a timbre-invariant manner.
  • The work on single rhyme embedding is not fully complete but can be used to validate the decoupling effect of Decoupled Components Features.
  • In the table below, Speaker denotes the first reference speech and Prosody denotes the second reference speech.
  • DecoupledSynth denotes a zero-shot TTS using only Speaker as reference speech.
  • DecoupledSynth (Prosody) then denotes the result of zero-shot TTS using Speaker with Prosody embedded in para-linguistic features.
